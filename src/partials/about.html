About Humanitarian ID

Humanitarian ID is a way to help humanitarian responders find, connect, and collaborate at the time of a humanitarian crisis (protracted or sudden onset). By making this process faster and smoother, the responders have more time to focus on what is needed most: saving lives and alleviating suffering.

Once registered, simply check into a country / emergency and specify your contact details for the contact list. You are always in control. You choose what details are available. When you are no longer responding, simply check out and your details will automatically be removed.

We require that all of those who register and make use of the solution behave according to our Code of Conduct.

We guess that if you have landed on this page you may have questions. Rest assured, we have an FAQ page that addresses the most common questions that we receive. Ranging from checking into multiple emergencies to explaining verified users to detailing how to have a missing organization added to requesting one's information be removed, we hope that the FAQ page will answer your question. If not, do not be afraid to contact us.

The Humanitarian ID solution has been created and is maintained by the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA). OCHA freely provides the solution to the humanitarian community.

If you would like to find out more about the project, join our mailing list, read our FAQs, or simply contact us.
